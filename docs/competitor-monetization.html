How the competition makes money.

A sourced breakdown of the monetization strategy of the bankruptcy petition-prep and practice-management tools we compete with — the pricing model, the headline numbers, and the second revenue line most of them run underneath the subscription. Who the competitors are is already documented in business-plan.html §2 and business-product-strategy.html §2; this document is the money deep-dive on that same set.

01

Monetization models at a glance

The category runs on three overlapping pricing logics. Which one a vendor picks says as much about who they're chasing as any feature does.

Model A
Per-seat / per-preparer

You pay for every person who touches the software. Revenue scales with the firm's headcount.

Who: Best Case (incumbent)6 — and Insolvia's own modeled price.13
Model B
Flat plan, scaled by filing volume

A flat annual/monthly fee tiered by how many cases you file, with users largely free. Revenue scales with case volume, not seats.

Who: NextChapter,1 CINcompass,7 Jubilee.9
Model C
Opaque / custom-quoted

No public price; a rep quotes per firm by case volume. Common at both ends — the incumbent and the AI-native challenger.

Who: Best Case,12 Glade AI.4
The second revenue line — payments

Several competitors don't just sell software; they take a cut of the money the firm collects from its clients. Glade builds in native payments to automate retainers and payment plans;5 Jubilee integrates LawPay;10 and MyCase (our channel) bundles LawPay free.3 Payment processing is a monetization lever independent of seat count — and one Insolvia's current seat-only model does not yet touch.

03

Competitor by competitor

The monetization detail behind each headline, with the reliability of each figure noted honestly. Two vendors — the incumbent and the AI-native threat — deliberately hide their numbers, so those entries are ranges and second-hand reports, flagged as such.

Best Case by Stretto

The incumbent to displace — ~80% of U.S. filings
MODEL · per-preparer subscription, desktop-rooted + cloud · sales-led, no public price
How they charge
By the preparer (per named user), the classic desktop-licensing shape carried into a subscription.6 Pricing is not published — you contact Stretto for a quote.1112
Figures on record
Our own business plan records desktop ~$1,750–4,500/yr + maintenance and Cloud ~$99/mo.13 A competitor (Glade) blog claims Best Case "starts at $2,395/month, with per-filing fees stacking at volume"5 — treat with caution: it's a rival's marketing, and it's an order of magnitude above the cloud figure, so it likely describes a large multi-preparer or bundled configuration rather than an entry price.
Second revenue lines
Maintenance/support fees on desktop; Stretto's broader business monetizes court/claims services on the corporate side.
Read for us
Opacity is a weakness we can attack with transparent, published pricing. Per-preparer billing punishes firms that grow headcount — the exact firms we want.

Glade AI

The real threat — AI-native, cloud-only
MODEL · flat monthly (unlimited users) + native payments · custom-quoted, opaque
How they charge
A flat monthly subscription that does not climb as you add users, custom-quoted per firm by case volume — sold explicitly as "scale your practice, not your team."4 No self-serve price is published.
The contradiction
Glade's own marketing elsewhere touts "transparent per-seat pricing,"5 which conflicts with the flat, unlimited-user framing that third-party reviews describe.4 The honest read: pricing is not actually transparent — it's quoted by a rep — and worth re-confirming directly before we rely on it.
Second revenue line
Native payments. Glade automates retainers and payment plans to cut 60–90-day collection cycles5 — monetizing the firm's client cash flow, not just seats.
Who they target
High-volume practices filing "well over a dozen cases a week" (50+/mo)4 — deliberately not our solo/small warm MyCase base.
Read for us
Their flat/unlimited-users model is genuinely attractive to multi-seat firms and undercuts per-seat billing on headcount. Our differentiation is segment (solo/small via MyCase) and channel — not price shape. Their opacity is, again, an opening for transparent pricing.

NextChapter

Modern cloud UX, weak AI
MODEL · flat annual plans tiered by case volume · users mostly free · fully published
How they charge
Flat annual plans priced by how many cases you file, not by seat. Every plan includes 6 users free; extra users are $99/user/yr.1
Plan ladder
Pro Basic $159 (≤4 cases/yr) · $649 (≤11) · $999 (unlimited). Pro+ $1,099 (≤11) · $1,599 (unlimited), adding client portal, texting, notices, scheduler. Whoa $1,999 (unlimited) adds Doc Creator.1
Add-ons
Chapter 13 $250/yr, Doc Creator $500/yr, MyChapter client portal $500/yr, Notices $200/yr, NextMessage $300/yr, Virtual Paralegal from $1,500/yr.1
Terms
No minimum contract, no setup or cancellation fee, free trial — "only pay when you download or file."1
Read for us
The most important comparison. A small firm with several preparers pays NextChapter one flat fee (6 users free) — while Insolvia's $129/seat/mo bills each of them. See §4.

CINcompass

Stretto's own cloud petition-prep
MODEL · all-inclusive monthly tiers by filing volume · unlimited users · published
How they charge
All-inclusive tiers scaled by monthly filing volume, with unlimited users included.78
Figures on record
Standard $75/mo (~$825/yr) up to 10 filings/mo; Plus $100/mo (~$1,140/yr) for 11–25 filings/mo.7 Dated ~2021; re-verify.
Read for us
Notable that Stretto's own cloud tool uses the opposite model to Best Case — volume-based, unlimited users — signalling where the cloud SMB market's price expectation actually sits.

Jubilee

LegalPRO Systems — the low-cost floor
MODEL · low flat monthly tiers by volume, or per-case · native payments (LawPay) · published
How they charge
Low flat monthly tiers by annual case volume, plus a per-case option and a free tier.910
Figures on record
Jubilee 36 from $50/mo · Jubilee 300 ~$83/mo · JubileePRO $71/mo (or $781/yr) incl. 51 filings/yr, payments, texting, notices · JubileeGo ~$59/case for two users.9 Free version + trial.
Second revenue line
Payments via a LawPay billing/payments integration.10
Read for us
Sets the market's price floor (~$50/mo). We do not compete here on price; we compete on channel, integration, and safe AI.

Caseway · CaseForm

In-channel signal — an AI add-on inside MyCase
MODEL · $49/user/mo add-on to MyCase · pay-as-you-go · published
How they charge
$49/user/mo as a MyCase add-on, month-to-month, no long-term contract; the standalone "Casey" research assistant is also $49/mo.3
Why it matters
Not bankruptcy-deep (generic court forms), so not a direct product threat — but it is proof MyCase partners rather than builds verticals, and a live benchmark for what an in-App-Bar add-on charges (~$49/user/mo). A signal to move fast in-channel.
04

Findings that touch our own model

Three things this research surfaced that bear directly on Insolvia's pricing and the figures in our own plan. Flagged for the strategy discussion — analysis, not settled decisions.

Finding 1 — our NextChapter willingness-to-pay anchor looks stale

The business plan anchors blended willingness-to-pay partly on "NextChapter ~$165/preparer/mo (~$1,980/yr)."13 But NextChapter's current published pricing is not per-preparer at all — it's flat annual plans by case volume ($159–$1,999/yr) with 6 users free.1 The per-preparer figure survives in some 2026 review write-ups6 but not on NextChapter's own page. If the seat-based anchor is wrong, the ~$1,700 blended WTP underpinning the SAM/ARR model deserves a re-check.

Finding 2 — the "MyCase Advanced = $89/mo for API" figure needs re-verifying

Our docs state the MyCase API needs the Advanced tier at $89/mo.13 MyCase's current published tiers are Basic $50 / Pro $100 / Advanced $130 per user/month billed annually.2 The $89 figure may be an older tier, a promotional rate, or a separate API line-item. It's a customer cost input and an App-Bar prerequisite, so worth confirming with our MyCase contact.

Finding 3 — we're the only SMB-facing tool billing per-seat

The modern cloud field — NextChapter, CINcompass, Jubilee — has converged on flat pricing by filing volume with users largely free.179 Glade goes further with flat/unlimited-users.4 Insolvia's $129/seat/mo13 matches the incumbent's per-preparer shape, not the challengers'. Worked example: a 3-preparer firm pays Insolvia ~$4,644/yr but NextChapter ~$999–1,999/yr flat (6 users free). Our channel + integration + safe-AI wedge has to carry that premium — or the packaging (flat tiers? seats bundled? a payments line?) is worth revisiting. Not a verdict; a tension to price in deliberately.
