01

Strategic thesis

Insolvia prepares, assembles, and e-files consumer bankruptcy cases — Chapters 7, 11, and 13 — for the solo and small firms that file the overwhelming majority of the ~574,000 U.S. bankruptcy cases filed each year, a non-discretionary market that grew ~11% in 2025 and was still climbing through Q1 2026.

Three things make this more than "another petition tool":

1 — A warm channel, not a cold start. We target firms already on MyCase, the cloud practice-management platform used by 19,000+ law firms where bankruptcy is a named practice area, reached through direct relationships and MyCase's public API and "App Bar" marketplace. Cold-start distribution is the single biggest killer of vertical software; we largely skip it.

2 — Integration is the wedge. Best Case is a Stretto walled garden that will never sync cleanly with a rival's practice-management system. By living inside the firm's MyCase workflow, Insolvia ends the double data-entry between practice management and a disconnected petition silo. A native desktop option remains for offline, keyboard-driven drafting — but the wedge is seamlessness, not desktop.

3 — AI where it is safe, deterministic where it must be. Claude extracts data from credit reports, pay stubs, and bank statements and reviews petitions for errors; the forms and means-test math stay rule-based and deterministic, so the numbers that get filed are never AI-generated. That directly answers the malpractice/hallucination fear slowing AI adoption in law.

We enter a ~$40M serviceable U.S. market for consumer petition-prep and e-filing, land first in the ~850-firm MyCase bankruptcy niche, and model a path to ~$5M ARR by Year 5. Every figure is built up and cited in the business plan.

The one-line thesis

Distribution × integration × safe AI. Reach firms through MyCase, live inside their workflow, and let Claude kill the data-entry — a corner the desktop incumbent and the cloud-only challengers can't occupy at once.

Why now

Filings are rising double digits, the incumbent has pointed its AI at the corporate side, and MyCase's open API plus frontier AI make a MyCase-native, AI-assisted product genuinely shippable today.

The moat — distribution × integration × safe AI

No single layer is defensible alone — but no competitor occupies all three at once. Out-AI the incumbent; out-distribute and out-integrate the AI-native startup.

Distribution — the MyCase channel

OUR BEACHHEAD

THE SURFACE
API + App Bar
MyCase's open API and marketplace let us integrate and be discovered in-product; API needs the Advanced tier ($89/mo).

Vertical SaaS lives or dies on customer-acquisition cost. Warm introductions plus an in-product marketplace collapse CAC and shorten sales cycles versus cold-selling bankruptcy software firm by firm. And once petition data flows automatically between MyCase and Insolvia, ripping us out means re-introducing double data-entry — the deeper the integration, the higher the retention, a moat Best Case structurally can't build into a rival's PM.

Safe AI — deterministic where it must be

Deterministic filing core

Forms and the means-test calculation stay rule-based. Claude does extraction and review; it never generates a number or legal text that gets filed. "AI does the typing; the law does the math" — the direct answer to hallucination-driven sanctions.

MyCase-context-aware AI

Because we integrate, Claude sees the firm's actual case and client data from MyCase — richer extraction and pre-filing review than a standalone tool like Glade can manage.

Enterprise data handling

Claude runs via Anthropic's API, which does not train on API data and offers zero-retention terms for qualifying use. We position that plainly — not a false "data never leaves the office" claim.

Malpractice insurance, reframed

A review agent cross-checks schedules vs. means test vs. SOFA, flags missing creditors, exemption errors, and undisclosed transfers against the district's local rules — sold as "a second set of eyes on every petition."

Customer & positioning

Beachhead: solo and small (2–15 seat) consumer-bankruptcy firms already running MyCase, currently bolting a disconnected Best Case onto their practice and re-keying data between the two.

Jobs to be done

  • 1Intake a client's full financial picture quickly and accurately, without re-typing what's already in MyCase.
  • 2Produce compliant petitions, schedules, and the means test with minimal re-keying.hair-on-fire
  • 3E-file to the court (CM/ECF) and retrieve case numbers and receipts.hair-on-fire
  • 4Track deadlines, court notices (PACER), and Proof-of-Claim events.
  • 5Bill, manage trust accounting, and keep matters organised — ideally without leaving MyCase.

The urgent job is #2 + #3 with confidence in compliance — a wrong or stale form is a filed error that risks money and sanctions. Insolvia owns that job first (AI extraction + deterministic forms + a review agent), then expands outward into notices and practice management.

07

Product compliance & the staleness obligation

A product truth, not a legal appendix: for a filing product the real risk is not missing features — it is shipping stale ones. Drawn from the regulatory source register.

The real product risk is staleness, not features

The feature list for a bankruptcy tool is knowable by reading the statutes and court specs. The hard part is that the inputs move: dollar thresholds, income tables, and expense standards change on fixed schedules. A correct product that goes unmaintained becomes a wrong one on a known date.

State exemptions & local court variation are the hidden iceberg

Federal forms are only half the surface. Exemptions vary by state and local rules vary by district (94 federal jurisdictions), and both must be versioned and auditable — this is why the forms engine is "compliance-forward" with a published update cadence, not a static template pack.

The maintenance calendar — an ongoing obligation, not a one-time load

Three inputs refresh on scheduled cadences with effective-date fields: the §522 dollar amounts (adjusted every 3 years), Census median-income figures (2–4× per year), and the IRS expense standards (periodically). These are recurring data pipelines the means-test and forms engines depend on — infrastructure of their own, and a commitment to attorneys that what they file is current.

The product decisions that shape the MVP

The MyCase spike is the go/no-go on the whole thesis

The entire GTM rests on the channel being real. So the first milestone proves it: one authenticated read and one write round-trip against MyCase. Access is de-risked — a founder's business partner works at MyCase — so this is fast, but the answer still matters: if write endpoints for the fields intake needs don't exist, the wedge changes shape. Far better discovered now than after building around it.

Web-first; desktop is built but not promoted

We push customers to the web app. Both desktop targets stay built on one Flutter codebase and installable, but they are not led with — no code-signing procurement yet. Desktop is the answer if attorneys refuse to leave the desktop habit: held ready, not the pitch. This removes the longest procurement lead time and preserves the Flutter optionality at almost no cost.

Safe AI is a compliance stance, not just a feature

Confining Claude to extraction and review — never generating a filed number or legal value — is the product's answer to the malpractice fear that slows legal-AI adoption. It is also why a server-side trust boundary is non-negotiable: we handle SSNs and full financials under GLBA Safeguards, so no client ever holds credentials or talks to a data service directly.

Migration is an onboarding lever, not an afterthought

Claude parsing Best Case exports on day one turns the incumbent's data lock-in into our fastest path in. Timed to renewal cycles, it lowers the switching cost that otherwise protects an 80%-share incumbent.

What the foundation MVP is really proving

The foundation isn't the product — it's the proof the delivery pipeline and the channel both work: infrastructure live end-to-end on web (and desktop kept green in CI), and the MyCase integration demonstrated read-and-write. Everything customer-facing — intake, extraction, the forms engine — is deliberately staged behind that proof.

Strategic risks

The business and product risks most likely to bite — named honestly, with mitigations. Engineering-execution risks live with their issues; these are the ones that shape the strategy.

Platform dependency (MyCase)
High
We'd be a tenant on MyCase's land — API terms, pricing, and partner favouritism are theirs; they could pick another bankruptcy partner or build it. The warm channel currently rests on a personal relationship, which moves with the person.Mitigation: deep integration + a formal partnership (not just warm intros), and a second channel (direct/NACBA) from day one so no single platform is existential. Confirm the relationship's nature before leaning on it in a raise.
Forms accuracy
Existential
A wrong or stale form is a filed error. This is the staleness obligation in §7, seen as a risk.Mitigation: versioned, auditable, deterministic forms pipeline with a published update SLA — never AI-generated filing values.
AI hallucination / UPL
High
Generative legal AI hallucinates on ~1 in 6 queries; courts are sanctioning AI-fabricated content.Mitigation: confine Claude to extraction/review; deterministic core for anything filed; no legal-advice surfaces.
Data security & PII
High
We handle SSNs and full financials under GLBA Safeguards and state law; AI adds a processor.Mitigation: server-side trust boundary, encryption in transit/at rest, least-privilege, audit logging; Anthropic API with no-training + zero-retention terms.
MyCase write coverage
Medium
Access is de-risked, but whether the API exposes write endpoints for the fields intake needs is a property of the surface, not the relationship. Practice-management APIs are often generous on read and thin on write.Mitigation: answer it first, cheaply — the documentation spike and a write round-trip in the MyCase milestone, before intake is designed around it.
Web-first is a bet on attorney behaviour
Medium
The market is described as desktop-loyal. Pushing web is right, but it's an assumption worth testing explicitly rather than discovering late.Mitigation: cheap to test with the design-partner firm — the desktop build exists and is kept green in CI, so reversing is a marketing + certificate decision, not a rebuild.
Incumbent + AI-native rivals
Medium
Stretto's data/court relationships are real; Glade is ahead on pure AI.Mitigation: compete on the intersection — channel, integration, and safe AI — not any single axis.
