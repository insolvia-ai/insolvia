Insolvia · Company Business Plan

The bankruptcy engine, native to the practice.

Insolvia is an AI-assisted, MyCase-native bankruptcy case-preparation and e-filing platform for consumer-bankruptcy law firms. We reach firms through a warm MyCase distribution channel, integrate directly into the practice-management system they already run, and use Anthropic's Claude to gut the data-entry that makes petition prep slow — displacing the aging desktop incumbent, Best Case by Stretto, on experience and workflow rather than price alone.

01

Executive summary

Insolvia prepares, assembles, and e-files consumer bankruptcy cases — Chapters 7, 11, and 13 — for the solo and small firms that file the overwhelming majority of the ~574,000 U.S. bankruptcy cases filed each year,1 a market that grew ~11% in 2025 and was still climbing through Q1 2026.1

Three things make this more than "another petition tool":

1 — A warm channel, not a cold start. We target firms already on MyCase, the cloud practice-management platform used by 19,000+ law firms8 where bankruptcy is a named practice area. MyCase exposes a public API and an "App Bar" marketplace,9 and we have direct relationships there. Cold-start distribution is the single biggest killer of vertical software; we largely skip it.

2 — Integration is the wedge. Best Case is a Stretto walled garden that will never sync cleanly with a rival-adjacent practice-management system. By living inside the firm's MyCase workflow, Insolvia ends the double data-entry between practice management and a disconnected petition silo. A native desktop option remains for offline, keyboard-driven drafting — but the wedge is seamlessness, not desktop.

3 — AI where it is safe, deterministic where it must be. Claude16 extracts data from credit reports, pay stubs, and bank statements and reviews petitions for errors; the forms and means-test math stay rule-based and deterministic, so the numbers that get filed are never AI-generated. That directly answers the malpractice/hallucination fear slowing AI adoption in law.15

We enter a ~$40M serviceable U.S. market for consumer petition-prep and e-filing (derived in §5), land first in the ~850-firm MyCase bankruptcy niche, and model a path to ~$5M ARR by Year 5 — every figure built up and cited below.

The one-line thesis

Distribution × integration × safe AI. Reach firms through MyCase, live inside their workflow, and let Claude kill the data-entry — a corner the desktop incumbent and the cloud-only challengers can't occupy at once.

Why now

Filings are rising double digits,1 the incumbent has pointed its AI at the corporate side,14 and MyCase's open API9 plus frontier AI make a MyCase-native, AI-assisted product genuinely shippable today.

Bankruptcy is a named vertical 8

Bankruptcy is one of the rare software markets that grows when the economy weakens. Total filings rose ~11% in the twelve months ending December 2025 (574,314 total; 549,577 non-business) and were still up ~12% through Q1 2026 (591,850 for the 12 months ending March 31).13 The surrounding software category — bankruptcy management tools — is growing at a ~16% CAGR globally ($1.22B in 2025 → $1.42B in 2026, projected $2.58B by 2030).4 Demand is legally mandated and recurring, which suits per-seat SaaS.

Note on the $1.42B figure That global "bankruptcy management tools" market 4 is far broader than our product — it includes corporate restructuring and claims-administration services. We use it only as directional context for category growth, not as our TAM. Insolvia's addressable market is built bottom-up in §5.

The incumbent — Best Case by Stretto

Best Case prepares an estimated ~80% of U.S. filings.13 Critically, its parent Stretto is pouring AI investment into the corporate side — the "Research Suite" and "Conductor" platforms for Chapter 11 restructuring professionals, built on a proprietary 6M-document corpus and Anthropic models.14 Best Case itself remains primarily form-automation software with only light AI assistance.14 The consumer petition-prep base — our target — is the part being left behind on AI.

Why the channel matters more than any feature

Vertical SaaS lives or dies on customer-acquisition cost. Warm introductions plus an in-product marketplace collapse CAC and shorten sales cycles versus cold-selling bankruptcy software firm by firm.

Integration as switching cost

Once petition data flows automatically between MyCase and Insolvia, ripping us out means re-introducing double data-entry. The deeper the integration, the higher the retention — a moat Best Case structurally can't build into a rival's PM.

04

AI strategy & moat

AI-native is fast becoming table stakes, not a differentiator — Glade is already there.12 Our edge is not "we use Claude" (so does everyone — Stretto 14 and MyCase's own "IQ" AI 10 included); it's how and where we apply it.

01 · the guardrail

Deterministic filing core

Forms and the means-test calculation stay rule-based. Claude does extraction and review; it never generates a number or legal text that gets filed. "AI does the typing; the law does the math" — the direct answer to hallucination-driven sanctions.15

02 · the differentiator

MyCase-context-aware AI

Because we integrate, Claude sees the firm's actual case and client data from MyCase — richer extraction and pre-filing review than a standalone tool like Glade can manage.

03 · the honest posture

Enterprise data handling

Claude runs via Anthropic's API, which does not train on API data and offers zero-retention terms for qualifying use.16 We position that plainly — not a false "data never leaves the office" claim.

04 · the review agent

Malpractice insurance, reframed

An agent cross-checks schedules vs. means test vs. SOFA, flags missing creditors, exemption errors, and undisclosed transfers against that district's local rules — sold as "a second set of eyes on every petition."

The moat is the intersection

MyCase distribution + deep integration + context-aware, deterministic AI. No single layer is defensible alone — but no competitor occupies all three at once. Out-AI the incumbent; out-distribute and out-integrate the AI-native startup.

Revenue potential & financial model

Built bottom-up from public data. Each figure below shows its derivation and cites its source. These are an illustrative model, not audited projections — the assumptions are stated so they can be pressure-tested.

Step 1 — How many firms & seats?

Derivation · addressable firms & seats There is no clean public count of U.S. consumer-bankruptcy firms (NACBA's 4,000+ members 7 are only the organized subset). We infer it from filings:
• 533,949 consumer filings (CY2025) ÷ ~60 cases/firm/yr ≈ ~8,900 firms 1 — the 60-cases/firm figure is our assumption for a blended solo+small practice (~1+/week).
• Seats: ~8,900 firms × ~2.5 preparer seats/firm ≈ ~22,250 seats (solo = 1; small firms 3–15; 2.5 blended).

Step 2 — What is a seat worth?

Derivation · blended willingness-to-pay Anchored to observed competitor pricing: Best Case Cloud ~$99/mo (~$1,188/yr) 5 and NextChapter ~$165/preparer/mo (~$1,980/yr) 6; Best Case desktop + maintenance runs higher.5 We take a blended market willingness-to-pay of ~$1,700 / seat / yr. Insolvia's own list price is set below that at $129/seat/mo = $1,548/yr to undercut NextChapter and the blended Best Case cost.

Firms already pay $99–$4,500/yr for this exact job.5 We re-platform an existing budget line, not create one — the easiest revenue to win.

A warm, not cold, beachhead

Landing inside MyCase's base with warm intros makes the Year 1–3 numbers a matter of execution, not of solving distribution from scratch.

Rising volume lifts every seat

Adjacencies raise the ceiling

Billing, trust accounting, notices, and Proof-of-Claim tracking are upsell modules that lift revenue per firm above the base seat over time.

06

Target customer & jobs-to-be-done

Beachhead: solo and small (2–15 seat) consumer-bankruptcy firms already running MyCase, handling Chapters 7, 11, and 13 — currently bolting a disconnected Best Case onto their practice and re-keying data between the two.

  1. 1Intake a client's full financial picture quickly and accurately, without re-typing what's already in MyCase.
  2. 2Produce compliant petitions, schedules, and the means test with minimal re-keying. hair-on-fire
  3. 3E-file to the court (CM/ECF) and retrieve case numbers and receipts. hair-on-fire
  4. 4Track deadlines, court notices (PACER), and Proof-of-Claim events.
  5. 5Bill, manage trust accounting, and keep matters organized — ideally without leaving MyCase.

The urgent job is #2 + #3 with confidence in compliance — a wrong or stale form is a filed error that risks money and sanctions. Insolvia owns that job first (AI extraction + deterministic forms + a review agent), then expands outward into notices and practice management.

Risks & compliance

The channel that de-risks distribution introduces a concentration risk of its own. Named honestly, with mitigations.

Platform dependency (MyCase)
High · new
We'd be a tenant on MyCase's land — API terms, pricing, and partner favoritism are theirs; they could pick another bankruptcy partner or build it.11 Mitigation: deep integration + a formal partnership, and a second channel (direct/NACBA) from day one so no single platform is existential.
Forms accuracy
Existential
A wrong or stale form is a filed error. Mitigation: versioned, auditable, deterministic forms pipeline with a published update SLA — never AI-generated filing values.
AI hallucination / UPL
High
Generative legal AI hallucinates on ~1 in 6 queries; courts are sanctioning AI-fabricated content.15 Mitigation: confine Claude to extraction/review; deterministic core for anything filed; no legal-advice surfaces.
Data security & PII
High
We handle SSNs and full financials under GLBA Safeguards and state law; AI adds a processor. Mitigation: encryption in transit/at rest, least-privilege, audit logging; Anthropic API with no-training + zero-retention terms.16
CM/ECF & PACER
Medium
Court-by-court e-filing is procedurally involved. Mitigation: build to the IEPD/CM/ECF spec early; stage rollout district by district.
Incumbent + AI-native rivals
Medium
Stretto's data/court relationships are real; Glade is ahead on pure AI.12 Mitigation: compete on the intersection — channel, integration, and safe AI — not any single axis.
