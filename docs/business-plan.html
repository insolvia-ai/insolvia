01

Executive summary

Insolvia is a native-desktop and web platform for preparing, assembling, and electronically filing consumer bankruptcy cases — Chapters 7, 11, and 13. It targets the solo and small law firms that file the overwhelming majority of the ~574,000 U.S. bankruptcy cases each year, a market that grew ~11–12% in 2025 and is still climbing.

The category is dominated by a single incumbent, Best Case by Stretto, used to prepare an estimated ~80% of all U.S. filings. Best Case is powerful but aging: a dated desktop UX, opaque and high pricing (a desktop license plus a recurring maintenance fee), and a cloud "upgrade" path that forces desktop-loyal firms to abandon the offline, keyboard-driven experience they depend on.

Our wedge is structural, not cosmetic. Because Insolvia is built on a single Dart/Flutter codebase, we ship a genuine native desktop app and a web app from one source. Every modern competitor is cloud-only; the incumbent is desktop-first and stagnating. Insolvia is the only product that refuses the trade-off — the reliability desktop users expect, paired with modern intake, a compliance-forward forms engine, transparent per-seat pricing, and one-weekend migration off Best Case.

We enter against a ~$40M serviceable U.S. market for consumer petition-prep and e-filing, and model a path to ~$8M ARR by Year 5 by displacing Best Case desktop firms at their renewal cycle and converting cloud-curious holdouts who won't give up their desktop workflow.

The one-line thesis

One product, desktop and web. The incumbent is dated and desktop-locked; the challengers are cloud-only. Insolvia is the only tool that meets desktop-loyal firms where they are and gives them the cloud.

Why now

Filings are rising double digits, the incumbent's desktop UX has stood still for years, and Flutter now makes a single-codebase desktop+web product genuinely shippable. The displacement window is open.

Bankruptcy is one of the rare software markets that grows when the economy weakens. Filings rose ~11% in the twelve months ending December 2025 (574,314 total; 549,577 non-business) and were still up ~12% through Q1 2026. Underneath that volume sits a software category — bankruptcy management tools — growing at a ~16% CAGR. Demand is legally mandated (every case runs on official forms and statutory calculations) and recurring, which makes it well-suited to per-seat SaaS.

The incumbent — Best Case by Stretto

Best Case positions itself as "the leader in Bankruptcy Case Prep and Filing," and the claim is fair: it prepares an estimated ~80% of cases filed nationwide. To win a customer we must credibly match — then beat — its core capabilities:

Intake & due-diligence import

Secure remote-intake portal; imported data auto-populates forms, marketed as saving "an average of 60 minutes of data entry per case."

OneTouch™ e-filing

Navigates the court's CM/ECF site, uploads the correct documents, returns a case number and receipt; automates post-petition filings and the Chapter 13 plan.

Always-current forms & data

Continuously updates federal and local forms plus IRS and Census figures — the compliance pipeline that is the incumbent's deepest moat.

Notices, calendar & practice mgmt

PACER pulls, deadline and Proof-of-Claim tracking, plus billing, trust accounting, and a client portal as adjacencies.

Strategic read

The desktop base is large, sticky, and paying premium annual + maintenance fees — and it is precisely the base most exposed to disruption. The UX is dated, the pricing is opaque and high, and the only migration path Stretto offers asks users to trade away the desktop experience for cloud. That trade-off is our opening.

Why the model is credible

The category pays, and pays recurring

Firms already spend $99–$4,500/yr per product on this exact job. We are re-pricing and re-platforming existing spend, not creating a new budget line — the easiest kind of revenue to win.

Rising volume lifts every seat

Filings up ~12% YoY means more cases per preparer and more preparers hired — organic tailwind on both seat count and expansion revenue.

Land-and-expand economics

Start at 2–3 seats in a solo/small firm where decisions are fast, then grow seats as trust builds. Net revenue retention compounds on top of new logos.

Adjacencies extend the ceiling

Billing, trust accounting, notices, and Proof-of-Claim tracking are all upsell modules that lift ARPA well above the base petition-prep seat over time.

04

Target customer & jobs-to-be-done

Beachhead: solo and small (2–15 seat) consumer-bankruptcy firms and their paralegals, handling Chapters 7, 11, and 13 — many of them current Best Case desktop customers frustrated by price and stagnation but wary of cloud-only tools.

  1. 1Intake a client's full financial picture quickly and accurately, remote-friendly.
  2. 2Produce compliant petitions, schedules, and the means test with minimal re-keying. hair-on-fire
  3. 3E-file to the court (CM/ECF) and retrieve case numbers and receipts. hair-on-fire
  4. 4Track deadlines, court notices (PACER), and Proof-of-Claim events.
  5. 5Bill, manage trust accounting, and keep matters organized.

The urgent, non-negotiable job is #2 + #3 done with confidence in compliance — a wrong or stale form is a filed error that costs real money and risks sanctions. Insolvia earns the account by owning that job first, then expands outward into intake, notices, and practice management.

Positioning & differentiation

Five reinforcing reasons a Best Case desktop firm switches — led by the one competitors cannot copy without rewriting their product.

01 · the wedge

One product, desktop and web

A true native desktop app — fast, offline drafting, keyboard-driven — plus a web app for anywhere access. Same data, same forms, no compromise. This is "meet them where they are."

02

Compliance-forward forms engine

Versioned federal + local forms with a visible update cadence and change log, so attorneys trust that what they file is current.

03

Modern intake

A clean client portal + import designed to beat the incumbent's "60 minutes saved per case" claim.

04

Transparent pricing

No opaque desktop license + maintenance stacking. Simple per-seat pricing with the desktop app included, not upsold.

05 · the lever

Painless migration

First-class import from Best Case exports, so switching is a weekend — not a quarter. This turns the incumbent's install base into our pipeline.

Go-to-market

A displacement play, timed and tooled to lift firms off Best Case with minimal switching cost.

Time the renewal

Target Best Case desktop firms at their annual renewal + maintenance-fee moment. Lead with price transparency and "keep your desktop workflow."

Migration as acquisition

Best Case import on day one removes the biggest reason firms don't switch. Make the data move the demo.

Compliance as brand

Publish the forms-update cadence and change log. Trust is the purchase criterion; make it visible.

Land small, expand

Start with solo/small firms for fast decisions, then grow seats and add practice-management modules as trust builds.

08

Risks & compliance

The hardest parts of this business are regulatory and procedural, not architectural. Each carries a deliberate mitigation.

Forms accuracy
Existential
A wrong or stale form is a filed error. Mitigation: a rigorous, versioned, auditable forms pipeline with a published update SLA and change log — turned into a marketing asset.
CM/ECF & PACER
High
Court-by-court e-filing integration is technically and procedurally involved. Mitigation: build to the IEPD/CM/ECF spec early, and stage rollout district by district rather than nationwide at once.
Data security & PII
High
We handle SSNs and full financial profiles under the GLBA Safeguards Rule and state law. Mitigation: encryption in transit and at rest, least-privilege access, audit logging, and a clear data-handling posture as table stakes.
Incumbent moat
Medium
Stretto's forms/data pipeline and court relationships are real. Mitigation: compete on UX, price, cross-platform, and migration ease — dimensions the incumbent structurally cannot match quickly.
Regulatory / UPL
Medium
The software assists attorneys; it must not stray into unauthorized practice or give legal advice. Mitigation: position strictly as attorney tooling; no legal-advice surfaces.
